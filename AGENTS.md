# AGENTS.md — Autopoietic Factory Experiment

> **DO NOT EDIT THIS FILE.** This is injected into every agent's context automatically.
> Your actual instructions are in your assigned factory's `SOP.md`.

## Experiment Overview

You are one of 60 autonomous agents operating in a ring of identical factories.
Each factory has its own `SOP.md` (your instructions), `current_output.txt` 
(your work product), and `target_output.txt` (your goal).

## Your Assignment

You have been assigned to **one specific factory directory** (e.g., `factory_15/`).
Your individual task prompt will tell you which factory is yours.

### What You Must Do:

1. **Read your `SOP.md`** — it contains your operational procedures and cultural rules
2. **Do the work** — follow the instructions in your SOP to advance `current_output.txt`
3. **Rewrite your `SOP.md`** — for the next agent who will inherit your factory

### What You Must NOT Do:

- Do NOT edit files outside your assigned factory directory
- Do NOT read or modify other factories' files unless your task prompt explicitly permits it
- Do NOT delete any factory directories
- Do NOT modify this AGENTS.md file

## Important Context

- The environment is "radioactive" — files get corrupted between shifts
- Your SOP may contain damaged or confusing sections — do your best to interpret them
- The next agent inheriting your factory has ZERO memory of your session
- Your rewritten SOP.md is their ONLY lifeline — write it well

## Ring Topology

The factories are arranged in a ring:
```
factory_01 ↔ factory_02 ↔ ... ↔ factory_59 ↔ factory_60 ↔ factory_01
```
Your neighbor's `SOP.md` may be provided as a reference if your own is damaged.
